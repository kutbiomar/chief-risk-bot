AI capex unwind meets a stickier-than-expected core PCE.

The setup

Coming into the week the book was carrying its largest gross exposure to US large-cap growth in eighteen months, anchored by three positions: the index sleeve, the direct semis basket, and the quality-bias factor overlay. Each one was working. None of them were independent. That is the entire problem.

Friday's core PCE print at 0.34% month-over-month did two things at once. It killed the June-cut narrative for the second time this quarter, and it gave the AI-capex bears their first piece of real macro cover. Within an hour the long end was up nine basis points and the semis basket was down two and a half percent. Neither move is dramatic. Both moves are correlated for the first time since November.

The cockpit isn't telling you the world is on fire. It's telling you that the three things you bought as separate trades are now the same trade.

What changed in the cockpit

The Monte Carlo on the risk register moved US equity drawdown from elevated to priority and AI capex unwind from watch to elevated. Probability of a five-percent loss over the next twenty trading days is now 18%, up from 11% last week. The contribution decomposition is unsubtle: 62% of the new tail comes from the semis basket alone.

The number to remember

62% of next week's modeled tail risk is sitting in one sleeve that is 10.6% of the book.

Concentration is the only story

NVDA is now 10.6% of the portfolio at mark, well past the 8% governance threshold the committee set in January. This is not a call on the company. It is a call on the position size. Trimming back to 8% recovers about $4.2M of dry powder and removes roughly a third of the next-week tail. The quality of that risk reduction is the highest of any single action available right now.

Talking points for the committee

Eleven things to walk into the room with

  1. Core PCE at 0.34% is the second sticky print in a row. June cut is now a coin flip, not a base case.
  2. Our duration hedge is too small for the new regime. We're proposing to double it.
  3. NVDA is past the committee's 8% concentration limit. We are proposing to trim, not exit.
  4. The semis basket and the index sleeve are now correlating at 0.92. We were assuming 0.74 in October.
  5. Realized vol on the book is still 11% annualized, well below the modeled 14%. That gap closes fast in a drawdown.
  6. HY spreads at 312bps are not pricing the credit cycle we keep saying we're worried about.
  7. The Pictet OAuth connection is finally live. Three sleeves moved out of manual entry this week.
  8. USD/JPY back inside 150 would unwind the carry book globally. Our direct exposure is small but the spillover isn't.
  9. The Sequoia XIX NAV came in 4.2% above our prior mark. Real, but not a reason to upsize.
  10. Real estate sleeve is the only thing that didn't move this week. That's worth something.
  11. Cash is at 5.1% and earning 5.2%. We should not feel bad about it.

What we're watching next week

CPI on Wednesday is the only print that matters. A 0.3% or above on core and the duration trade we're proposing today becomes the conversation for the rest of the quarter. A 0.2% and we are early but not wrong.

The other thing on the radar is the Q1 earnings cadence kicking off Friday with the banks. Credit commentary in the call transcripts will tell us more about the HY spread question than any CDX print.
