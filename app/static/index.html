The product

A reading room, not a trading terminal.

Most risk products were built for sell-side desks. ChiefRiskBot was built for the person preparing the Monday memo for the family principal.

01

A weekly briefing that reads like Pictet's, not Robinhood's.

Editorial-quality memo every Monday: lede, talking points, recommended actions, audit footer. Print-ready PDF in one click. Reviewed by you before it goes to the committee.

02

A live cockpit with the four things that actually matter.

Composition, value-at-risk with event markers, ranked risk register with Monte Carlo, mitigation tree. Updated every morning from your custodians. Same data Claude reads.

03

Custodian APIs and PDFs, in the same table.

Connect Schwab, Pershing, Pictet, IBKR via OAuth. Upload Julius Baer statements as PDFs and Claude extracts the positions. Manual entries for art, real estate, and direct deals.

04

Concentration limits the committee can trust.

Set HHI thresholds, single-name caps, and asset-class bands. Get flagged the day a position breaches them, not the quarter after.

05

Audit log a Big Four signs off on.

Every action by every member, every API call, every Claude generation, append-only and hash-chained. Export to your auditor in CSV or Avro. SOC 2 Type II from day one.

06

Single-tenant. Your data, your encryption keys.

Each client gets an isolated workspace and an isolated database. BYOK encryption available on the Enterprise tier. Your custodian credentials are never shared across tenants.

How it works

From custodian to committee in five steps.

Most CIOs are reading their first real briefing within two hours of signing in.

01

Connect your custodians

OAuth into Schwab, Pershing, IBKR, Pictet, Carta, and a dozen others. Read-only, read-only, read-only. Five minutes per source.

02

Upload the things that aren't on an API

Drop in Julius Baer statements, Sequoia capital account reports, Knight Frank appraisals. Claude extracts positions and ties them to your table.

03

We build your cockpit

Composition, VaR, risk register, mitigation tree. Reconciled to your most recent statement. Concentration limits set to your committee's actual policy.

04

Claude writes the briefing

Every Monday at 6am workspace time. Same model that wrote the briefing this page links to. Reviewed by you before it goes anywhere.

05

You walk into the room ready

Eleven talking points. Three priced actions. One memo the principal actually reads.
